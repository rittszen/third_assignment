About Imran Khan

Imran Khan, born on October 5, 1952, in Lahore, Pakistan, is one of the country’s most iconic personalities.
Known first as a world-class cricketeror also known as a
COOL PLAYER .
Then as a philanthropist, and later as a political leader, his life reflects determination, resilience, and a deep commitment to national service.
Imran Khan is a Pakistani politician and former international cricketer.
He served as the 22nd Prime Minister of Pakistan from 2018 to 2022.
Before politics, he was the captain of the Pakistan cricket team and led them to victory in the 1992 Cricket World Cup.

EARLY LIFE AND CRICKET CARRER

IMRAN KHAN
studied at Aitchison College in Lahore and later at Oxford University in England. His cricket career began in the early 1970s, and he quickly rose to become one of the finest all-rounders in the sport. His greatest sporting achievement came in 1992 when he captained Pakistan to its first and only Cricket World Cup victory, cementing his place as a national hero.

Political Career

Imran Khan founded the political party Pakistan Tehreek-e-Insaf (PTI) in 1996.
He became Prime Minister after the 2018 general elections.
